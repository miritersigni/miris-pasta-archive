Category

Long Pasta


Region

Emilia-Romagna



Tagliatelle are long, flat ribbons similar to fettuccine but slightly thinner. This type is made from durum wheat semolina and eggs, resulting in a rich, golden colour. The dough is rolled out into thin sheets, then cut into strips of the desired width. Their name comes from the Italian verb “tagliare,” meaning “to cut,” reflecting their method of preparation. Tagliatelle pair well with hearty sauces like ragù, as well as lighter options such as cream-based or simple tomato sauces.
