Fusilli is a short, spiral-shaped pasta characterized by its helical or corkscrew form. Traditionally, this shape was achieved by twisting thin strips of pasta dough around a rod or spindle, resulting in a tightly coiled structure. Modern production methods often involve extrusion through dies to create the spiral shape. The spirals of fusilli are designed to capture and hold onto sauces effectively, making it a versatile choice for various dishes, from simple tomato-based ones to richer meat or cream sauces.
